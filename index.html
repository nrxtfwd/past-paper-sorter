// how it works

Three steps from messy stack to clean topical sets.

No accounts, no manual tagging. Just upload, wait, download.

01

Upload your stack

Drop in past paper PDFs and (optionally) their mark schemes. Bulk uploads via .zip work too — up to 100 files at once.

02

We classify each question

Every question is parsed and tagged by topic. Optionally narrow down to a single topic — useful if you only need atomic structure or kinematics today.

03

Download two PDFs

One file with topical questions, another with the matching mark schemes. Properly formatted, faithful to the source, ready to print or share.

// what's inside

Built like the tools you actually use.

Same friction-free feel as a background remover or audio converter — just pointed at your past paper backlog.

Bulk & .zip uploads

Two clearly separated lanes — papers on one side, mark schemes on the other. Compressed archives are unpacked automatically.

Topic-perfect grouping

Questions are sorted into clean topical buckets so students can drill the exact area they're weakest on, instead of grinding entire papers.

Results in under five minutes

Most batches finish in two. Larger ones get a live progress bar so you know exactly how much longer there is to wait.

Faithful to the source

Question formatting, figures, and numbering are preserved. What you upload is what you get back — just resorted by topic.

Pick one topic — or all

Just want kinematics? Filter to a single topic and skip everything else. Useful for last-minute targeted revision sets.

Works on any device

Designed mobile-first. Sort papers from a school computer, a phone on the bus, or a tablet at the kitchen table.
